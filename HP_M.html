Exploring Haptics in Gaming for the Visually Impaired

Using the concept of audio+haptic data exchange between user and system, we intend to create a gaming console that is light, easy to use, portable and reprogrammable to provide visually impaired people a new source of entertainment for them, thereby enhancing their lives.

We have used an arduino along with a blutooth sensor, vibration motors, rubber caps etc. to develop a gaming console fo the visually impaired.The basic idea revolves around providing the user with an input apart from voice. We studied various arcade games and styles of games to understand the feasability of its implementation on to a tactile feedback equiped console. We finally decided to implement the egg-catcher game. The user will connect the gaming console with an android application with the help of bluetooth. The application will provide the user the interface to navigate through the console as well provide audio feedback.

User Study and Iterative Prototyping

We started our research by conducting user interviews at IIIT and the National Association for the Blind (NAB) in order to understand the current gaming scenario for the visually impaired. We then developed a cardboard prototype and conducted usability studies Further, we developed a second cardboard prototype in order to incorporate ergonomics and conducted usability studies Next, we started to assembling the working prototype by deciding the hardware layout and getting the device 3Dprinted. We further carried out usability study for the working protoype. Based on the final feedback we refined our working prototype and came up with the final device. Lastly, we conducted evaluation for the same.
